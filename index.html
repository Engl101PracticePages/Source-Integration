Model (Example EMT paragraph + rules)

Use this as a structure model. Notice: quote marks for the quote, paraphrases are truly in the writer’s own words, and every borrowed idea has a citation.

Being an EMT or paramedic means dealing with emergencies and helping people right away. The Bureau of Labor Statistics says EMTs and paramedics “provide emergency medical care” and sometimes also transport patients to medical facilities (Bureau of Labor Statistics). It also explains that the job can be stressful and physically hard, and that workers may have to work nights, weekends, and holidays (Bureau of Labor Statistics). To get into this field, people usually need training after high school, and every state requires EMTs and paramedics to be licensed (Bureau of Labor Statistics). In Washington, the CareerOneStop license page says you need a Department of Health credential and you have to pass exams, plus do continuing education to keep your license active (CareerOneStop). A job posting for Cape Fox in Seattle also shows what employers want because it requires a current EMT-B certification and Basic Life Support (BLS) certification (Cape Fox Concentric Methods). The posting also says they want 1–2 years of experience responding to medical events, which makes it clear that experience matters too (Cape Fox Concentric Methods). Overall, these sources show that becoming an EMT is not just about wanting to help people, you need training, licensing, and the right certifications to qualify for jobs.
